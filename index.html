About

Making complex things click for people

I'm a training facilitator and instructional designer from Bandung, Indonesia. Over the past six years, I've worked with corporate teams, students, and community groups on learning that changes behaviour rather than just adding information.

My work tends to sit around personal finance, personal productivity, and professional development. Those topics matter to me because they sit close to everyday decisions, habits, and performance.

My background in Psychology from Universitas Indonesia (GPA 3.77) shapes how I design. I pay attention to how people learn, where they get stuck, and what helps the material feel usable. Across my work I've built 50+ behavioural assessments, written 20 e-learning scripts, and designed 50+ interactive learning activities.

Design philosophy

What I believe about good learning

These are the ideas I keep coming back to, regardless of the topic or format. They shape every decision I make as a designer.

Behaviour over knowledge

Knowing something and doing something are completely different things. I design with the second one as the goal, and work backwards from there.

Psychology first

Motivation, cognitive load, retrieval practice, spacing effects. I use what we know about how people learn, not what feels intuitive.

Structure creates freedom

A clear learning architecture gives people room to explore and make connections. Without it, even good content tends to get lost.

Ship, learn, improve

A tested first version beats a perfect one sitting in a folder. I'd rather get something in front of real learners and refine it from there.

Involve learners early

The people who will use the learning know things I don't about their context, their language, and what gets in the way. I try to bring them in before the design is locked.

Measure what matters

Completion rates don't tell you much. I look at whether people can apply what they learned and use that to keep improving the design.
