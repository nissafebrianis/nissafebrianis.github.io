About me

Making complex things click for people

I'm a training facilitator and instructional designer from Bandung, Indonesia. For the past six years I've been designing and facilitating learning for corporate teams, students, and community groups. The kind that actually changes what people do, not just what they know.

The topics I spend most of my time on are personal finance (budgeting, saving, managing money well), personal productivity (well-being, habits, sustainable performance), and professional development (leadership, communication, career growth). I find them genuinely interesting, which I think shows in the work.

My background is in Psychology from Universitas Indonesia (GPA 3.77), so how I design is grounded in how people actually learn, not how we assume they do. Across my work I've built 50+ behavioural assessments, written 20 e-learning scripts, and designed 50+ interactive learning activities.

My design process
1
Discover

Needs Analysis

Before anything else, I spend time understanding who the learners are, what gaps actually exist, and what success looks like in practice. It sounds obvious, but skipping this step is where most training goes wrong.

2
Design

Learning Architecture

I map learning objectives to activities, select the right formats (e-learning, live session, game), and sequence content for maximum retention.

3
Develop

Build & Script

Scripts, assessments, activities, and materials, all designed with psychology principles in mind to drive engagement and real behaviour change, not just completion rates.

4
Deliver

Facilitate & Iterate

I facilitate live and digital learning, collect data, and refine based on learner feedback and performance outcomes.

Module 03 · Design Philosophy
My philosophy

What I believe about good learning

These are the ideas I keep coming back to, regardless of the topic or format. They shape every decision I make as a designer.

🎯

Behaviour over knowledge

Knowing something and doing something are completely different things. I design with the second one as the goal, and work backwards from there.

🧪

Psychology first

Motivation, cognitive load, retrieval practice, spacing effects. I use what we actually know about how people learn, not what feels intuitive.

📐

Structure creates freedom

A clear learning architecture gives people room to explore and make connections. Without it, even good content tends to get lost.

🔄

Ship, learn, improve

A tested first version beats a perfect one sitting in a folder. I'd rather get something in front of real learners and refine it from there.

🤝

Involve learners early

The people who will use the learning know things I don't about their context, their language, and what actually gets in the way. I try to bring them in before the design is locked.

📊

Measure what matters

Completion rates don't tell you much. I look at whether people can actually apply what they learned and use that to keep improving the design.
