Dasar-Dasar Blockchain

A self-paced module designed for the general Indonesian public to understand blockchain in plain language : starting with ledger basics and moving into everyday use.

The context

Blockchain and cryptocurrency have generated enormous interest among the Indonesian public, but quality educational resources in Bahasa Indonesia are scarce. Most content is either too technical (English-language whitepapers) or too promotional (social media influencers).

I designed this self-paced module as a personal project to bridge that gap. The goal was to produce a resource that explains blockchain in plain Indonesian, starting from what a ledger is, all the way to how people use blockchain in everyday contexts.

What stood out

The gotong royong analogy for consensus was what readers responded to most: it made an abstract systems concept feel like something they already understood intuitively.

The problem

Two barriers. First, language: high-quality blockchain education is mostly in English, which excludes a large segment of the Indonesian audience. Second, prior knowledge: readers come from wildly different backgrounds. Some have never heard of Bitcoin, others have dabbled in trading but don't understand the technology.

The module needed to serve both groups without boring either. That meant layered explanations, analogies grounded in local contexts, and a structure that lets readers skip sections they already know.

Test your knowledge

Click each question to reveal the answer. These are questions real readers asked.

Apa bedanya blockchain dengan database biasa?
Blockchain adalah database terdistribusi di mana data dicatat dalam blok yang saling terhubung secara kriptografis. Tidak ada satu entitas pun yang mengontrol seluruh data, dan perubahan pada blok sebelumnya akan terdeteksi oleh seluruh jaringan.
Apa itu consensus mechanism?
Mekanisme konsensus adalah cara semua pihak dalam jaringan blockchain setuju tentang state data yang benar. Analogi gotong royong: semua warga harus sepakat tentang aturan bersama sebelum membangun. Dalam blockchain, mekanismenya termasuk Proof of Work dan Proof of Stake.
Apakah blockchain selalu lebih aman?
Tidak selalu. Keamanan blockchain tergantung pada implementasi, konsensus mechanism, dan ukuran jaringan. Blockchain publik besar seperti Bitcoin relatif aman karena biaya serangan sangat tinggi, tapi blockchain kecil atau private bisa rentan. Teknologi bukan jaminan mutlak.

My approach

I structured the module into five parts:

1. Dari Buku ke Blockchain. Starts with a physical ledger (buku catatan) and introduces the concept of a distributed ledger before ever mentioning cryptocurrency.

2. Cara Kerja. Covers how blockchain actually works: hashing, consensus mechanisms, mining, using analogies from Indonesian daily life (gotong royong for consensus, for example).

3. Mata Uang Digital. Introduces Bitcoin, Ethereum, and the idea of digital scarcity. Focuses on understanding the trade-offs between different cryptocurrencies rather than investment advice.

4. Penggunaan Sehari-hari. Covers real applications: remittances, supply chain tracking, digital identity, and DeFi, with Indonesian case studies where available.

5. Resiko dan Masa Depan. A balanced look at risks (volatility, regulation, scams) and what the future might hold, designed to leave readers informed rather than sold.

Every module uses perbandingan (comparison tables), analogi (analogies with local references), and ringkasan (one-page summaries at the end).

Design decisions

Bahasa Indonesia throughout. Every word is in Indonesian. Technical terms are introduced in English with Indonesian explanations, then consistently referred to in Indonesian.

Local analogies. Blockchain consensus is explained through gotong royong (mutual cooperation). Mining difficulty is compared to finding the right "kunci" (key) in a traditional lock. These cultural references make abstract concepts feel concrete.

Modular, skip-friendly. Each module works as a standalone read. A "sudah tahu?" (already know?) box at the start of each section lets advanced readers skip ahead.

No investment advice. The module explicitly avoids price discussion, trading strategies, or "how to get rich" framing. The goal is understanding, not speculation.

What I learned

"Designing in Bahasa Indonesia forced me to think harder about clarity. When you can't rely on English technical vocabulary, every concept has to earn its explanation. The local analogies, especially gotong royong for consensus, were the thing readers responded to most. It reminded me that the best explanation is the one that uses what the learner already knows."
